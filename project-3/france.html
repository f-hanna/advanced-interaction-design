The Rococo Period

Early 1700s - Mid 1700s

The French Rococo period emerged after the Baroque era, known for its ornate and playful aesthetic, mirroring France's period of peace and prosperity.

This era revolved around the aristocracy, who moved from Versailles to Paris, embracing leisure and indulging in romantic intrigues, creating a culture of luxury and excess. This contrasted sharply with the majority of French society, as the aristocracy, a small fraction of the population, owned 90% the country's wealth. Resentment from the common people grew, sowing the seeds for a revolution.

Reflecting this wealth divide, Rococo art moved away from Baroque grandeur towards a whimsical style, characterized by asymmetry, delicate curves, pastel hues, and intricate ornamentation, embodying luxury and refinement.
